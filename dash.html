Changing Nature of University Admissions — Australia

Australia’s university admissions are evolving. The level of study, by state, by the balance of domestic and international students, and by gender. This visualisation traces those shifts from an overview big picture down to specific states and institutions so you can explore what changed, where did it change, and why did it change.

1) Course enrolment levels over time

Start with the time-series of course types. Use the legend and the year brush to highlight key periods and degree classifications. Look for inflection points (e.g., Bachelor decreasing while Postgraduate grows) and whether levels move with positive correlations. This chart shows the national overview.

2) Course mixtures - Compare years

Use the year vs year stacked normalised bars to compare a baseline with a chosen year. Because bars are normalised, you see share shifts immediately: which types of degrees are growing in relative popularity. Then jump back to the time-series to see how that change unfolded.

3) Enrolments by State

The map connects national proportions to each state. Hover to see State totals and percentage of national enrolments.

4–5) Domestic vs International

Read the above and below charts together: The stacked bars show each state’s year by year composition of domestic vs international students. The lines show the change over time, with the state selection being filterable.

6) Institution heatmap

This heatmap allows for easy analysis of domestic vs international enrolments at the institution level. Start by selecting a state or viewing all states. The width of each block represents total enrolments for that institution, while the split shows the domestic (bottom) and international (top) shares. Colour differentiates institutions or states. Use the year slider to see how these dynamics have evolved over the years.

7) Gender share of enrolments

The gender donut summarises who is enrolling in the chosen year. Read it alongside level shifts and state patterns to see whether growth areas are balancing or widening gender representation.

Summary — bringing it together

The changing landscape of University enrolments comes into focus: How the student mix is shifting, where people are studying, and what they are studying. Use this visualisation to explore across time and location to separate temporary swings from practical trends. Consider how these shifts might influence future university enrolments.
